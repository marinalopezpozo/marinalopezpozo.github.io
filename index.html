Marina López-Pozo, PhD

Postdoctoral scientist in the field of plant ecophysiology. Lover of science, biology, and above all, of everything that makes this world greener, more fertile and more accessible to mankind.

Expert in the study of plant organisms in extreme environments, on Earth and beyond. I developed my Doctoral Thesis between my beloved land full of greenery and sea, and the most inhospitable corners of the world. I continued to pursue and fulfill my dreams by enrolling at the University of Colorado in a NASA-funded project for the development of space crops.

Beyond Earth, I am currently part of the research team that will try to take plants to the Moon. The limits are in our minds, and so are our dreams, and these have no end.

Expertise

  • Plant kingdom environmental stress ecophysiology (water, light, temperature, CO2)
  • Photosynthesis and chlorophyll fluorescence
  • Isolation and analysis of pigments, lipids, proteins, ROS and antioxidants
  • Biomass and healthy product production
  • International collaboration in over five countries
